Pathogen & Disease

Pathogen: Mycobacterium tuberculosis
Disease: Tuberculosis (TB)

How It Causes Disease

TB is caused by inhaling airborne droplets containing M. tuberculosis. Once in the lungs, the bacteria can multiply and evade immune detection by residing inside macrophages. (CDC, 2023)

Symptoms

Common symptoms include chronic cough, fever, night sweats, weight loss, and fatigue. In more severe cases, coughing up blood can occur. (WHO, 2022)

Immune Response

The immune system responds by sending macrophages and T-cells to the infection site, forming granulomas to contain the bacteria. However, the pathogen can persist in a latent state. (Mayo Clinic, 2023)

Treatment

TB is treated with a combination of antibiotics over several months, including isoniazid, rifampin, pyrazinamide, and ethambutol. (CDC, 2023)

Prevention

Preventative measures include the BCG vaccine, wearing masks in high-risk areas, and ensuring good ventilation. Early detection and treatment of latent TB are also crucial. (WHO, 2022)
